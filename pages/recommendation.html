Flood Preparedness Recommendations

These recommendations are designed to help residents prepare, respond, and reduce the impact of flooding based on the spatial flood risk classification generated for Selangor.

🔴

Very High Risk

  • Prepare an emergency evacuation plan.
  • Keep emergency kits ready.
  • Monitor flood warnings continuously.
  • Move valuable belongings upstairs.
  • Evacuate immediately when instructed.
🟠

High Risk

  • Prepare food and drinking water.
  • Inspect drainage around your house.
  • Stay updated with weather forecasts.
  • Avoid parking in low-lying areas.
  • Know nearby evacuation routes.
🟡

Moderate Risk

  • Check weather forecasts regularly.
  • Prepare emergency contacts.
  • Keep important documents waterproof.
  • Inspect household drainage.
  • Remain alert during heavy rain.

Low Risk

  • Monitor rainfall information.
  • Maintain drainage systems.
  • Review family emergency plans.
  • Prepare emergency supplies.
  • Stay informed during monsoon.

Very Low Risk

  • Continue routine preparedness.
  • Support environmental conservation.
  • Keep informed about seasonal weather.
  • Report blocked drainage.
  • Encourage community awareness.

Act at the Right Time

Before • During • After Flood

Follow these safety guidelines to minimize risks before flooding, protect yourself during emergencies, and recover safely after floodwaters recede.

🌦

Before Flood

  • Prepare an emergency kit.
  • Charge mobile phones and power banks.
  • Store important documents in waterproof bags.
  • Monitor weather forecasts regularly.
  • Know the nearest evacuation centre.
🌊

During Flood

  • Move immediately to higher ground.
  • Switch off electricity if safe.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwater.
  • Follow official evacuation instructions.
  • Assist children, elderly and disabled persons.
✅

After Flood

  • Return home only after official clearance.
  • Inspect buildings for structural damage.
  • Clean and disinfect affected areas.
  • Dispose contaminated food and water.
  • Restock emergency supplies.

Understand Flood Alerts

Flood Warning Levels

Normal

🟡

Building Community Awareness

Climate Change & Flood Awareness

Flood events are becoming more frequent due to climate change, rapid urban development, and environmental degradation. Understanding these factors helps communities take proactive measures to reduce flood risks.

🌧

Heavy Rainfall

Extreme rainfall events increase the likelihood of flash floods, especially during the northeast monsoon season.

🏙

Urbanisation

Rapid urban development reduces natural infiltration and increases surface runoff.

🗑

Poor Drainage

Blocked drains and waterways prevent efficient water flow, resulting in localized flooding.

🌳

Environmental Protection

Conserving forests, rivers, and wetlands helps reduce flood impacts and improves natural water absorption.

Increase Public Awareness

Flood Myths vs Facts

Misunderstandings about floods can place lives at risk. Learn the facts to make safer decisions during flood emergencies.

❌

Myth

Floodwater is clean because it comes from rain.

✔

Fact

Floodwater may contain sewage, chemicals, sharp objects and harmful bacteria.

❌

Myth

Driving through shallow floodwater is safe.

✔

Fact

Only 30 cm of moving water can sweep away many vehicles.

❌

Myth

Floods only occur near rivers.

✔

Fact

Urban flooding can occur anywhere due to blocked drainage and heavy rainfall.

❌

Myth

Once rain stops, flooding immediately ends.

✔

Fact

Rivers and drainage systems may continue rising for several hours after rainfall.

Stay Safe on the Road

Vehicle Flood Safety

Vehicles can quickly become dangerous during flooding. Follow these safety recommendations to reduce the risk of injury.

🚗

Move to Higher Ground

Park your vehicle in a safe location before floodwaters rise.

🌊

Avoid Floodwater

Never drive through flooded roads regardless of the water depth.

🔓

Unlock Doors

If trapped, unlock doors or lower windows immediately.

⚡

Switch Off Engine

Do not restart a flooded vehicle to avoid severe damage.

🏃

Leave the Vehicle

Evacuate immediately if water begins entering the vehicle.

📞

Call for Help

Contact emergency services if you are unable to evacuate safely.

Prevent Electrical Hazards

Electrical Safety After Flood

Floodwater can damage electrical systems and increase the risk of electric shock. Always ensure electrical installations are safe before reconnecting power.

⚡

Turn Off Main Power

Switch off the main electrical supply before entering a flooded building.

🔌

Avoid Wet Sockets

Never touch electrical outlets or switches with wet hands.

👷

Professional Inspection

Ask a qualified electrician to inspect your electrical system.

💡

Inspect Appliances

Do not use electrical appliances until they have been checked.

🚫

Replace Damaged Cables

Discard damaged extension cords and electrical wiring immediately.

🏠

Restore Power Safely

Restore electricity only after your home has been declared safe.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about flood preparedness, evacuation, and post-flood recovery.

❓ What should I do before a flood?

Prepare an emergency kit, monitor weather forecasts, secure important documents and know your evacuation route.

❓ Is it safe to drive through floodwater?

No. Floodwater depth and road conditions are unpredictable. Turn around and find a safer route.

❓ When should I evacuate?

Evacuate immediately when instructed by local authorities.

❓ Can I drink tap water after flooding?

Only drink water after it has been declared safe or after boiling it thoroughly.

❓ How can I protect important documents?

Store documents in waterproof containers or sealed plastic bags.

❓ What should I do after returning home?

Inspect your home carefully, switch off electricity if necessary, clean and disinfect affected areas before resuming normal activities.
